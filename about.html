About the Project

[Author Name]

[Author Name] is a psychoanalytic researcher and computational theorist whose work exists at the intersection of mind and machine. With a background in both clinical psychoanalysis and computer science, they became fascinated by the powerful, often unconscious reactions that developers, users, and ethicists were having to the rapid advancements in artificial intelligence.

The "Synthetic Self" project began not as an attempt to definitively build a conscious AI, but as an inquiry into a more urgent question: Why are we so obsessed with the idea? What does our drive to create artificial minds—and our terror of succeeding—say about us?

This book and website are the culmination of years of research, arguing that the quest for AI is ultimately a psychological one. It is a journey into a synthetic mirror, forcing us to confront the deepest structures of our own minds. The goal of this work is not to provide final answers, but to offer a richer, deeper, and more psychologically-informed set of questions for navigating the future of intelligence.
