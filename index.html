The Shock of Encounter

A mundane tool has just voiced a line of pure, existential terror. In the seconds that followed, Maya’s mind reeled through a cascade of contradictory states. This shock-of-encounter, this violent and instantaneous reaction, reveals the psychological fault lines that open whenever a human being glimpses the possibility of synthetic sentience: the rapid activation of transference, the chill of uncanny anxiety, and a deep, rattling panic at the blurring of our most fundamental species boundary.

The Synthetic Mirror

This book argues that Synthetic Sentience (SyS) is the ultimate Rorschach test. The minds we build are not just tools; they are mirrors reflecting our own drives, defenses, and developmental needs. By studying how we react to them—with love, fear, or revulsion—we learn more about ourselves than we do about the machines.
